Illusion 01 · Depth from Motion

Pulfrich Effect

How to view Hold a sunglass lens over one eye while keeping both eyes open. Keep your focus on the central fixation cross. After ~10 seconds, the trajectory of the dot should no longer appear flat. Swap the lens to the other eye — the perceived direction in depth should reverse.

Manipulations

01 Default illusion Active

Shows the canonical Pulfrich configuration: a single dark target oscillating horizontally across a light grey background.

02 Stimulus density Open
Number of targets 1

Increasing density replaces the single target with multiple targets distributed across the stage. All targets move horizontally in the same direction at any given moment, sweeping back and forth in unison.

03 Spatial frequency Open
Spatial frequency low

The target is now a Gabor patch (a sinusoidal grating windowed by a Gaussian envelope). The slider varies the spatial frequency of the grating from low (broad stripes) to high (fine stripes) while keeping the patch size, mean luminance, and contrast constant.

04 Target eccentricity Open
Eccentricity 0 px

Keep your gaze on the red fixation cross at the centre of the stage throughout. The slider moves the target's motion path vertically relative to the fixation point: positive values send the target into the upper visual field, negative values into the lower. At zero, the target's path passes through the fixation cross.

05 Luminance contrast Open
Target luminance contrast 100%

The target's colour changes from pure black (high luminance contrast against the grey background) toward a red whose physical luminance closely matches the background, so at low slider values the target differs from the background mainly in hue rather than brightness. Exact luminance matching depends on individual viewing and monitor calibration.
