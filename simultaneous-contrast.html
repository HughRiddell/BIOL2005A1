Illusion 03 · Lightness

Simultaneous Contrast

How to view The two grey patches (targets) are the same luminance - that is the same gray colour - but are surrounded by different luminance backgrounds. What do you notice about the percieved luminance of the two targets?

Manipulations

01 Default illusion Active

Shows the canonical simultaneous contrast configuration: two physically identical grey patches placed on opposing dark and light surrounds.

02 Surround articulation Open
Articulation 0

Increasing articulation adds randomly sized and randomly shaded rectangles to the light (right) surround. The base luminance of both surrounds and the grey value of the two central patches remain unchanged.

03 Remote region luminance Open
Outer region luminance 50%

The slider varies the luminance of the outer region around the right-hand patch. The central patch and its immediate surround are held constant; only the more distant background changes. The left-hand configuration is held constant as a reference.

04 Ring split Open
Split offset 0%

The slider pulls the two halves of the ring apart vertically. At zero, the ring is a single unbroken annulus; at maximum, the upper and lower halves have separated and slid across the dark/light boundary in opposite directions.
