Illusion 02 · Motion

Motion Aftereffect

How to view This illusion has two phases: a dynamic adapter phase in which the rings will move for 20 seconds and a static test phase in which the stimulus does not move. Fixate the red cross at the centre throughout both adaptation and test. Do not let your eyes follow the moving pattern, or the aftereffect will be weakened or absent. Press Start trial to begin.
READY Press Start to begin a trial.

Manipulations

01 Default illusion Active

Shows the canonical motion aftereffect: a 20-second adaptation phase of inward-contracting concentric rings, followed by a static test pattern of the same rings.

02 Test contrast Open
Test pattern contrast 100%

The adapter is unchanged (full-contrast contracting rings). The slider varies only the contrast of the static test pattern, from very faint (5%) to full contrast (100%).

03 Test position offset Open
Horizontal offset 0 px

The adapting stimulus is shown in its usual central location. After adaptation, the static test pattern is presented at a horizontal offset from that location, ranging from far left (negative) through centred (zero) to far right (positive). Keep your gaze on the central fixation cross throughout both phases.

04 Counterphase adapter Open
Counterphase balance 0%

During adaptation, two ring patterns are superimposed: one contracting and one expanding. The slider sets the balance between them. At 0%, only contracting rings are visible. As the slider increases, an outward-moving pattern is progressively added until at 100% the two are perfectly balanced.

05 Noise test lifetime Open
Noise lifetime static

The adapter is unchanged. The test stimulus is a random-noise pattern. The slider controls the noise lifetime: at zero, a single noise pattern is generated and shown statically for the whole test phase. As the slider increases, the noise is regenerated at progressively shorter intervals, until at maximum the pattern is regenerated on every frame.
