Illusion 04 · Form Perception

Illusory Contours

How to view Observe the four "pac-man" inducers (black circles with a wedge cut out). There are no other other shapes drawn on the screen, but what do you see?

Manipulations

01 Default illusion Active

Shows the canonical Kanizsa configuration: four black pac-man inducers at the corners of an imaginary square, each oriented so its missing wedge faces the centre.

02 Support ratio Open
Inducer radius 80 px

The inducer centres are held in fixed positions at the corners of the imaginary square. The slider varies only the radius of each pac-man inducer, changing how much of the imaginary square's edges is physically defined by the inducer boundaries.

03 Inducer relatability Open
Inducer rotation 0°

All four inducers are rotated about their own centres by the same angle. At zero rotation each pac-man's wedge points toward the centre of the imaginary square; as the slider increases, all four pac-man mouths rotate progressively away from this canonical alignment.

04 Contrast polarity Open
Polarity inversion 0%

The background is a mid-grey. The slider sweeps the luminance of two diagonally opposite inducers from black (matching the other two), through grey, toward white. The other two inducers remain black throughout.

05 Wedge colour saturation Open
Saturation 0%

The four pac-men retain their canonical position, orientation, and black-on-white configuration. The slider tints a thin arc along each pac-man's curved wedge boundary: at zero, the arc is white (matching the background and effectively invisible); as the slider increases, the arc progressively takes on a red hue.
